Budget Gaming PC

Jan 26th, 2014

This is a budget gaming rig I built for a friend of mine who was in desperate need of an upgrade, and this is quite an upgrade to his almost decade-old laptop that could only run the simplest of indie games. Now he can enjoy the full spectrum of games he’s bought on steam and doesn’t have to worry if they will run or not.

As you can tell by this picture of the build after its first POST, I’m no photographer. But I still think it looks good :D

The Parts List

  • NZXT Source 210 S210-001 Black Computer Case With Side Panel Window
  • EDIMAX EW-7811Un USB 2.0 Wireless nano Adapter
  • Rosewill HIVE Series HIVE-550 550W, Modular Design, Active-PFC Power Supply
  • ASUS DVD-Writer Black SATA Model DRW-24F1ST
  • AMD FX-6300 3.5GHz (4.1GHz Turbo) Socket AM3+ Six-Core Desktop Processor
  • Seagate Barracuda ST1000DM003 1TB 7200 RPM 64MB Cache SATA 6.0Gb/s 3.5” Internal Hard Drive
  • ASRock 960GM-VGS3 FX Micro ATX AMD Motherboard
  • G.SKILL Ripjaws Series 4GB (2 x 2GB) 240-Pin DDR3 SDRAM DDR3 1333 (PC3 10666) Desktop Memory
  • XFX R7-260X-ZNJ4 Core Edition Radeon R7 260X 1GB 128-bit GDDR5 PCI Express 3.0 CrossFireX Support Video Card

And this is a picture of the 260X. Is rly nice. Works good too.

The build went pretty well, although the CD/DVD burner from ASUS ended up being a dud. I picked up another one for him at a hardware store and other than some driver issues (I’m looking at you AMD), it was smooth sailing from there!

Total Build Price: 578.00$ ish, crazy Quebec tax and shipping included.
